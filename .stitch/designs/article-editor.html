SEBI UPDATE warning High Impact Effective: May 1, 2024

SEBI Mandates New Disclosure Norms for FPIs

summarize

Key Changes at a Glance

  • Enhanced granular details required for ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs).
  • Strict 7-day timeline for reporting material changes.
  • Exemption criteria tightened for specific sovereign wealth funds.

Overview

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has issued a critical circular mandating enhanced disclosures for Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs). This move aims to prevent potential circumvention of Minimum Public Shareholding (MPS) requirements and opportunistic takeovers using the FPI route.

Granular Disclosure Triggers

FPIs are now required to provide detailed granular data concerning ownership, economic interest, and control up to the level of all natural persons if they meet specific criteria:

  1. Holding more than 50% of their Indian equity AUM in a single corporate group.
  2. Holding over INR 25,000 crore of equity AUM in Indian markets.

Exemptions

Certain entities are exempt from these granular disclosures, provided they do not share a common beneficial owner with non-exempt FPIs:

  • Government and Government-related investors.
  • Public Retail Funds.
  • Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs).
